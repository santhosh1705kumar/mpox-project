Educational Content - Monkeypox

About Monkeypox

Monkeypox is a rare viral disease that occurs mainly in central and western Africa. It is similar to smallpox but less severe. Monkeypox can cause a severe rash, fever, and other symptoms.

Symptoms

  • Rash (often begins on the face then spreads to other parts of the body)
  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Muscle aches
  • Swollen lymph nodes

How it Spreads

Monkeypox can spread through close contact with infected animals (usually rodents) or humans. It can also spread through respiratory droplets or contact with contaminated objects.

Prevention Tips

  • Avoid contact with animals that may carry the virus
  • Wash hands frequently with soap and water
  • Avoid touching or handling animals or their products without protection
  • Practice good hygiene habits
  • Stay informed about outbreaks and follow public health guidelines
