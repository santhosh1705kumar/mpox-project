Monkeypox FAQs

What is Monkeypox?

Monkeypox is a rare viral disease that occurs mainly in remote parts of Central and West Africa, near tropical rainforests. It can cause a severe illness in humans, similar to smallpox but milder.

How is Monkeypox Transmitted?

Monkeypox can spread to humans from animals such as rodents and primates, or through contact with bodily fluids of infected humans. It can also be transmitted from person to person through respiratory droplets or close contact with infected skin lesions.

What are the Symptoms of Monkeypox?

Symptoms of monkeypox include fever, headache, muscle aches, exhaustion, and lymph nodes swelling. The disease progresses to a rash that develops into characteristic pox lesions.

Is There a Treatment for Monkeypox?

There's no specific treatment for monkeypox, but supportive care and antiviral medications can help manage symptoms and reduce the risk of complications.

How Can Monkeypox be Prevented?

Preventive measures include avoiding contact with animals that could harbor the virus, practicing good hygiene, and getting vaccinated against smallpox if you live in or travel to areas where monkeypox is endemic.

Where Can I Get More Information?

For more information about monkeypox, its symptoms, transmission, prevention, and treatment, you can visit reputable health organizations such as the World Health Organization (WHO) or the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).
